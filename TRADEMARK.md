# Zaude™ Trademark Policy

**TL;DR:** Use Zaude all you want. Just don't call your fork "Zaude", and don't claim you built it.

---

## What this is

"Zaude" is an unregistered trademark (**™**) of Ziad Momen. This policy explains what you can and cannot do with the name "Zaude", the Zaude logo, and related branding. The code itself is separately licensed under [MIT](./LICENSE) — this policy governs only the **name** and **branding**.

Short version: the code is free. The name is not.

This policy exists because the MIT license protects the code but does **not** protect the project's identity. Without a trademark policy, someone could fork Zaude, make trivial edits, and ship it under the same name — confusing users and undermining the project. This policy prevents that specific failure while keeping the framework fully open to legitimate use, contribution, and forking.

## Permitted uses (no permission needed)

You can do all of these freely:

- **Use Zaude** on any number of personal or commercial projects.
- **Modify Zaude** for your own use — edit hooks, commands, patterns, docs.
- **Share your modifications** privately (with your team, in your company).
- **Write about Zaude** — blog posts, talks, tutorials, reviews, criticisms. Reference "Zaude" by name as much as you want when referring to **this** project.
- **Link to Zaude** — you don't need permission to link or cite.
- **Fork the repository** on GitHub. A public fork that still shows the Zaude name is fine as long as it's clearly a fork (GitHub's fork relationship is visible; the fork page says "forked from ziadmomen10/zaude").
- **Contribute back** — open PRs, file issues, propose changes. Contributions are welcome under the standard MIT-licensed terms in [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md).
- **Describe your project as "Zaude-compatible"** or **"based on Zaude"** or **"built on Zaude"** — these descriptive uses are allowed as long as they're accurate.

## Restricted uses (permission required)

These require explicit written permission from Ziad Momen (ziad.momen@ultahost.com):

- **Naming a distinct product "Zaude"** or any confusingly similar name (e.g. "Zaudee", "Zaude Pro", "Zaude Plus", "ZaudeJS", "Zaude for Cursor").
- **Using the Zaude logo** (the SVG in `assets/logo.svg`) on a distinct product, service, or marketing material that is not this project.
- **Selling a service called "Zaude"** (e.g. "Zaude Cloud", "Managed Zaude"), hosted or otherwise.
- **Registering domains containing "Zaude"** for commercial use (e.g. `zaude.com`, `zaude.io`, `zaude.dev`) unless you are Ziad Momen or have written permission.
- **Listing Zaude on a marketplace or plugin catalog** under a name implying you are the author or maintainer.

## Prohibited uses (not permitted under any circumstances)

These are never allowed, regardless of modifications:

- **Claiming to be the author or creator of Zaude.** You did not build this; you built on this.
- **Claiming that your fork is the official Zaude** or is sponsored/endorsed by Ziad Momen.
- **Replacing the Zaude name in your fork and shipping it as your own work** without disclosing that it is derived from Zaude. Call your fork something else and disclose the Zaude origin — that's fine.
- **Using "Zaude" in a way that disparages the project, the author, or the community** in bad faith.
- **Registering "Zaude" as a trademark** in any jurisdiction.

## If you fork and rebrand

The spirit of OSS is that you can take Zaude's code, change the name, and ship it. That is allowed under MIT.

The **required etiquette** (enforceable under the MIT copyright-notice clause in [LICENSE](./LICENSE)):

1. Keep the MIT copyright notice in all source files. You may add your own copyright alongside it — never replace it.
2. In your fork's README, include a clearly visible line stating the project is based on Zaude, in a form like:

   > Built on [Zaude](https://github.com/ziadmomen10/zaude) by Ziad Momen. This project is a derivative work and is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Zaude project.

3. Do not use the name "Zaude" or the Zaude logo in your fork's branding.

Do these three things and you can ship your rebranded fork freely. The code is yours to evolve.

Zaude credits upstream projects it integrates with (wshobson, VoltAgent, MCP server authors) in its [README](./README.md#-credits). When you contribute to Zaude, you agree that:

1. Your contribution is licensed under the same MIT license as Zaude's code.
2. You retain copyright on your specific contribution but grant Zaude the right to use it under MIT.
3. Zaude's trademark remains with Ziad Momen regardless of contribution volume.

This is the same model used by Linux, Python, Rust, and most healthy OSS projects. You own what you write; the project name stays stable.

## Enforcement

Zaude is protected as an unregistered trademark under common-law trademark rights in jurisdictions that recognize them (including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and most of Europe). The author may:

- Issue **cease-and-desist** letters for trademark violations.
- Request **DMCA takedowns** for clear violations on GitHub, npm, PyPI, app stores, or similar platforms.
- **Register the trademark** at USPTO (or equivalent) if widespread adoption warrants it. Registration has been evaluated and is currently held in reserve.

The author will **not** enforce against:

- Legitimate criticism, reviews, or editorial commentary.
- Personal or internal-team forks that don't publicly claim the Zaude name.
- Good-faith educational use (tutorials, courses, demonstrations).
